Chelsea Football Club

About Chelsea

Chelsea Football Club was founded in 1905 by Gus Mears in Fulham, London.



The club experienced initial success with a league title in 1955, followed by FA Cup wins in the 1970s. A major turning point was the acquisition by Roman Abramovich in 2003, which led to an era of signific ant trophy wins, including multiple Premier League titles and the Champions League.

Founding and early year

Foundation: Founded on March 10, 1905, after businessman Gus Mears acquired the Stamford Bridge athletics stadium.
Early success: The club quickly became popular, earning the nickname "The Pensioners". They had mixed fortunes, moving between divisions, but won their first league championship in 1954-55.

Periods of success and change


1970s: Won the FA Cup and the European Cup Winners' Cup.



2003 ownership: The club's fortunes changed dramatically after Russian billionaire Roman Abramovich bought the club.



2022 ownership: The club was sold to a Todd Boehly-led consortium.




2003-2022 era: This period saw Chelsea win numerous trophies, including five Premier League titles, five FA Cups, and two Champions League titles.

Key achievements and legacy


Trophy record: Chelsea has won numerous domestic and European honors and is one of the world's most successful clubs.
European success: Became the first London club to win the Champions League in 2012.
Current status: As of 2025, the club has also become the first to have won all four major UEFA men's club competitions.
